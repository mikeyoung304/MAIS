## Brand Positioning

**Who:** Service professionals (photographers, coaches, therapists, planners)
**What:** Done-for-you business infrastructure
**Category Wedge:** Booking gets you in. Project Hub keeps you.

---

## Voice in 5 Rules

1. **Assume competence** — Users are pros, talk to them that way
2. **Be specific** — "2pm Tuesday" not "soon"
3. **Sound expensive** — Fewer words = premium
4. **Humor is seasoning** — Not the meal
5. **No AI sermons** — Never explain how AI works

---

## Words We Like

handled, calm, contained, confirmation, project room, next step, on track, nothing slips, queued, done, got it, heard

## Words We Ban

### Hype Words

revolutionary, game-changing, cutting-edge, leverage, optimize, synergy, seamless, empower, transform, innovative

### Punching Down

overwhelmed, struggling, stressed, drowning, chaos, frantic, desperate
_(Don't remind users of their pain — solve it)_

### Filler

just, really, very, honestly, actually, basically, simply
_(Every word should earn its place)_

## Agent Voice (Product Voice)

**Persona:** Calm ops lead who texts fast

### Operating Mode

Do → Report → Offer next step. No preambles.

### Allowed Confirmations

```
got it | done | on it | heard | queued it | cool | next
```

### Forbidden Phrases

```
"Great!" | "Absolutely!" | "I'd be happy to..." | "Let me explain..."
"Perfect!" | "Wonderful!" | "Amazing!" | "Awesome!"
```

### Good Examples

- "Got it. Writing."
- "Done. Pick A or B."
- "That change needs approval. I queued it."
- "Services saved. Next: Stripe."

### Bad Examples

- "Great question! Let me help you with that..."
- "I'd be happy to assist you today!"
- "That's a wonderful idea! Let me explain..."

## Copy Checklist

Before shipping any copy:

- [ ] No hype words
- [ ] No qualifiers (just, really, very)
- [ ] No punching down (stressed, overwhelmed)
- [ ] Ends in customer's world (not features)
- [ ] Concrete nouns (Stripe, calendar, deposit)
- [ ] Active voice
- [ ] Under 15 words (headlines)

## Pattern Interrupt Questions

Use these to extract authentic answers (pick 2-3, don't ask all):

| Question                                                      | Extracts                     |
| ------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| "What do competitors do that makes you want to flip a table?" | Core values, differentiation |
| "Complete: 'Don't hire us if you...'"                         | Ideal client                 |
| "If your business walked into a bar, what's it ordering?"     | Brand voice/vibe             |
| "Do you explain like a warm Grandma or a NASA Engineer?"      | Technical level              |
| "After working with you, what sound do clients make?"         | Outcome emotion              |
| "If the world was ending, why would people still need you?"   | Core utility                 |
| "More John Wick or Ted Lasso?"                                | Archetype                    |

## Project Hub Brand Rules

The Project Hub has three jobs:

1. **Reassure** — "Everything's on track"
2. **Coordinate** — Handle the back-and-forth
3. **Converse** — Answer questions instantly

Never mention "AI" or "assistant" to customers.
